9:16 is the vertical aspect ratio used by Stories, Reels, and TikTok — and is the only aspect ratio that fills a portrait phone screen edge to edge. The "9" is the width and the "16" is the height, expressed as a ratio. On a 390 × 844 logical-pixel iPhone, 9:16 maps cleanly to the full screen.

Other aspect ratios are letterboxed when displayed in 9:16 surfaces. A 1:1 square creative shows with horizontal black bars above and below; a 4:5 nearly-portrait creative shows with smaller bars. Letterboxing reduces visual impact on fast-scroll surfaces because the eye registers the bars as "not native content."

The strategic implication: if your campaign includes 9:16 placements, plan native 9:16 creative. Don't rely on 1:1 or 4:5 fallbacks. The performance gap between native 9:16 and letterboxed creative is significant — typically 15–30% on engagement metrics.
