Primary text is Meta's name for the long-form caption that sits above the creative on Feed-style placements. It accepts up to 500 characters on Facebook Feed, up to 2,200 on Instagram Feed (matching organic captions), and up to 125 hard maximum on Stories. The visible window is 125 characters before See more on Feed, 90 on Stories, 72 on Reels.

Primary text is the field where new buyers feel safest writing because the limits are generous. It's also where most ad spend gets wasted because the visible window is much smaller than the hard maximum. Anything past the visible cutoff is read by a single-digit percentage of viewers.

The TikTok equivalent of primary text is "caption", with similar dynamics but a different visible cutoff (~100 characters). Both platforms preserve the post-cutoff text for indexing and search but rarely surface it to viewers.

## How to write inside the visible window

Lead with the hook in the first 60 characters. Use 60–110 for proof or offer. Treat 110–125 as deliberate cliffhanger space. Anything past 125 is bonus content, useful for SEO, not for conversion.
