Picking the right ad platform is the single highest-leverage decision a media buyer makes. The character-limit and safe-zone math is downstream — the platform choice has to be right first. Here is a quick decision matrix based on niche and intent.

**B2B / SaaS / professional services** — LinkedIn first. Sponsored Content for awareness, Document Ads for whitepaper distribution, Conversation Ads for self-segmenting prospects. Meta as a secondary for retargeting because Meta's targeting + lookalikes still beat LinkedIn's audience tools, but LinkedIn's first-touch quality is unmatched for B2B.

**Visual commerce / fashion / home / food** — Pinterest + Instagram. Pinterest for top-of-funnel save-and-return behavior; Instagram for mid-funnel reaction-and-comment. Pinterest pulls users in research mode; Instagram pulls users in scroll mode. Run both simultaneously.

**Vertical short-form video** — TikTok + YouTube Shorts + Snapchat Spotlight. TikTok for global scale and trend amplification, Shorts for US tier-1 viewer quality, Spotlight for younger demos. Plan caption strategy to YouTube's tighter ~60-char clamp so creative travels across all three.

**Community / niche knowledge** — Reddit. Subreddit-tone-matched Promoted Posts and Conversation Ads pull 2-3x typical performance in research-heavy subs (r/personalfinance, r/PPC, r/sysadmin, r/marketing).

**Brand awareness / reach** — YouTube Bumper + Masthead + Meta Reels. Frequency-driven recall plays.

**Direct response / impulse purchase** — Meta Feed + TikTok Shop + Snapchat Collection. Tight-funnel formats with native commerce integration.
