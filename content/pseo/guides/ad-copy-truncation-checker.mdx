An ad copy truncation checker answers one question before you spend money: **where does each platform clip this exact copy, and is anything important on the wrong side of the cut?** Ads Manager and the native preview tools don't answer it — they render full text. CaptionSnap does: paste the copy, see the real per-placement truncation point and the UI overlays that cover it.

## What a real checker has to do

A useful truncation checker is not a character counter. A counter tells you "138 characters". It does not tell you that Facebook drops "… See more" at ~125, that your headline clips at ~27, that TikTok's CTA covers the bottom of the frame, or that Pinterest shows only ~40 of a 100-character title. The check that matters is **visible vs. hidden against the live render**, per placement.

## How to use it

1. **Paste the exact copy** you're about to ship — not an approximation.
2. **Read only what shows before the cut.** If the hook or CTA is hidden, rewrite and re-check.
3. **Repeat per placement.** The same copy clips differently on Facebook Feed, Reels, TikTok, and Pinterest — check each surface you're running.
