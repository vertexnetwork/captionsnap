Previewing ad copy before you spend is the cheapest optimization in paid social, and almost nobody does it correctly — because the preview people use (Ads Manager, the native ad tools) renders full text and hides the one thing you need to see: **the truncation point.** A real pre-spend preview shows the live feed render, where the cut actually lands.

## The math of skipping it

A clipped headline doesn't fail loudly — it just quietly underperforms. On a $5,000 campaign, copy whose hook or CTA is hidden past "… See more" is roughly $200+ of impressions spent on text no one read, and the loss compounds every flight until someone notices. The preview costs ten seconds. The skip costs a percentage of every campaign.

## The pre-spend check

1. **Paste the final copy** — exactly what's going live, every variant.
2. **Read only the pre-cut text** on each placement in the plan. Hook present? CTA present? Sentence not split mid-thought?
3. **Fix and re-check** before the campaign is enabled, not after the first day's data looks soft.
