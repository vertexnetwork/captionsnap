If your Facebook ad text is showing a **"… See more"** link and the rest of your copy is hidden, nothing is broken — Facebook truncated your primary text at the feed's visible window, and the cut happens **before** the line you thought was visible. The Ads Manager preview does not show this cut; it renders the full text. The live feed does not. That gap is why the ad looked fine when you built it and clipped when it ran.

## Where the cut actually lands

Facebook Feed primary text collapses to roughly the first **125 characters** on mobile before "… See more". Your headline clips earlier — around 27 characters on many placements before the CTA strip covers it. Anything past those points is invisible to the ~80% of users who never tap "See more".

## How to fix it

1. **Move the hook and the offer into the first ~125 characters.** Treat everything after as bonus context, not load-bearing copy.
2. **Don't bury the CTA in the headline's tail.** Front-load it; the headline clips far earlier than the primary text.
3. **Check the live render, not Ads Manager.** Paste your exact copy into the simulator above and read it the way the feed will, before you spend.
