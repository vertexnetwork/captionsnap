If your TikTok ad caption looks fine in the upload preview but is **covered by the UI** in the live feed — hidden behind the username, the side action rail, or the CTA button — that's not a character-limit problem. It's a safe-zone problem. TikTok's caption can be well under the limit and still be unreadable because the platform's own interface sits on top of it.

## It's the furniture, not the count

TikTok In-Feed truncates the caption text at a character point, but the more common failure is spatial: the right-side action rail (like, comment, share, profile) and the bottom CTA cover the lower-right and bottom of every video. Caption text that extends into that region is rendered but visually obscured. The upload preview shows a clean frame; the live app does not.

## How to fix it

1. **Keep the hook in the upper-left safe zone.** The top-left is the only consistently unobstructed region.
2. **Assume the bottom ~14% and right ~20% are covered.** Design the caption and any burned-in text outside that.
3. **Preview against the real chrome,** not a blank video frame — the simulator renders TikTok's actual overlays so you see what's hidden.
