For an agency or in-house team shipping creative weekly, ad-copy truncation is not an occasional mistake — it is a recurring QA gap that costs client trust and wasted spend. This is the workflow that closes it without adding a tool nobody opens.

## Where truncation slips through

It slips through because the check happens in the wrong place: the designer checks in Figma, the buyer checks in Ads Manager, and **neither renders the live truncation point**. By the time the ad is in-feed, the spend has started and the clip is a client email, not a QA note. The fix is to move the check to a single, fast, pre-handoff step.

## The workflow

1. **Copywriter drafts to the visible window,** not the character max. The first line is the ad.
2. **Single QA gate before handoff.** Paste all variants into the checker; confirm hook and CTA clear the cut and the UI overlays on every placement in the media plan.
3. **Export the rendered proof.** Attach the PNG to the creative handoff so the buyer and client see exactly what the feed will show — no "it looked fine in the preview" disputes.
4. **Re-verify on spec changes.** Platforms move truncation points; a dated re-verification feed means the QA gate stays accurate without manual research.

The QA gate becomes ten seconds, not an afternoon, and it survives staff turnover because it's a step, not a person's habit.
