YouTube Skippable In-Stream Ads run before, during, or after host video content. The video itself does the heavy lifting — text on the ad lives in the companion banner that renders below the video player on mobile. The companion banner pulls a long headline (90 chars max, ~45 visible) and description (70 chars max, ~60 visible) from the underlying Google Ads asset bundle. The skip button enables after exactly 5 seconds of playback and lives in the bottom-right corner of the player.

The most expensive mistake on Skippable In-Stream is treating the companion banner as decoration. The companion stays visible for the entire ad duration on mobile and persists for several seconds after the video ends — so the headline and description are doing real work for click-through. Plan the companion text as if it were the only thing the viewer reads.

## What changed in 2026

YouTube extended the long-headline visible window from 40 to 45 characters in 2026 on iPhone 14-class devices but kept the hard cap at 90. The description hard cap moved from 60 to 70 characters in the same update.
