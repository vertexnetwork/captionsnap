TikTok captions accept 2,200 characters. That is the actual hard maximum. It's also a number that tricks new buyers into writing long-form ads that no one reads. Because the visible window is roughly 100 characters before TikTok appends "...more", the actual usable caption budget is ~95 characters of hook plus whatever you choose to hide behind the tap.

Tap-through rates on "...more" are even lower than Meta's See more taps. Aggregate published numbers from TikTok agency partners suggest 1–4% of viewers expand the caption — and that number drops to under 1% when the visible region doesn't earn the tap. In practical terms, anything past character 100 is invisible.

So why does TikTok offer 2,200 characters? Two reasons. First: parity with organic captions, where the limit was set generously to allow personal storytelling. Second: indexing — TikTok uses caption text for search and content matching, and a longer caption gives the algorithm more signal. So there's a defensible reason to write past 100 characters, but it's an SEO reason, not a conversion reason.

## The 100-then-SEO pattern

Use the first 100 characters for human readers. Use characters 100–500 for keyword-rich descriptions that help discovery. Use characters 500–2,200 for hashtag stuffing if your strategy benefits from it. Keep the human-facing message tight.
