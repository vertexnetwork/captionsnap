The TikTok side panel — the right-rail column with like, comment, share, bookmark, and audio icons — covers the right 14% of the canvas from approximately 28% Y down to 82% Y. That's a 14% × 54% rectangle, more than half the height of the right edge of the screen. It is the largest single overlay region on any TikTok placement.

Brands that compose product shots, logos, or copy on the right side of their TikTok creative routinely lose those elements behind the side panel. The side panel is also slightly translucent, so the obscured content remains visually present but unreadable. This is a worse failure mode than full occlusion — viewers see "something is there" but can't parse it, which reads as a low-quality ad.

The side panel does dim during initial impression for ~1 second of fade-in, but otherwise stays at full opacity throughout the ad. Treat it as always-visible.

## The 86% rule
