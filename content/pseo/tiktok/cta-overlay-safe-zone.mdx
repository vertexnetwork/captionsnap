TikTok's sponsored CTA strip sits at the very bottom of the canvas, covering the bottom 8% of vertical real estate across the full width. It contains the call-to-action button and the disclosure ("Sponsored", "Ad", or regional equivalent). It is always visible while the ad is on screen and cannot be dismissed by the viewer.

The 8% number sounds small but it's larger than you'd think on a 9:16 canvas. On a 390 × 844 logical-pixel iPhone, 8% is 67 pixels — enough to fully cover a CTA button you've composed into your video, plus the lower edge of any product shot you've placed near the bottom.

Compose creative against an upper bound of 92% Y. Anything below that line is either covered by the CTA strip or sitting directly above it where the eye is being pulled toward the platform-native CTA. Your composed CTA button — if you have one — should not exceed roughly 88% Y.
