TikTok TopView is the highest-CPM placement TikTok sells. It runs the moment the user opens the app — the first impression, full screen, sound on. Caption space caps at 100 characters total maximum (not 100 visible — 100 hard) and truncates visibly at roughly 60 characters. This makes TopView the tightest character clamp on TikTok.

The reason for the tighter clamp is that TopView is intentionally short-attention. The user opened the app to scroll, and TikTok is buying them a 5–60 second pause. Captions on TopView are a secondary read — the audio and visual are doing the work — and the platform reflects that by allocating less screen real estate to text.

The display name field caps at 40 characters and behaves the same as on In-Feed. The Skip button sits in the top-right at roughly 8% × 8% from the corner, which is rarely a creative concern unless you place text in that exact region.
