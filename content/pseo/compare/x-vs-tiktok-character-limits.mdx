X and TikTok approach character limits from opposite philosophies. X enforces a hard 280-character body cap on Promoted Posts (regardless of account tier as of 2026). TikTok allows 2,200 characters of caption — eight times longer — but clips at roughly 100 visible characters before "...more" on every placement.

The convergence is interesting: both platforms effectively give you ~100 characters of attention to work with. X's hard cap is your visible budget; TikTok's soft clamp is your visible budget. The difference shows up in how to think about copy structure: X copy is one tight statement (280 chars total). TikTok copy is a hook followed by hashtags, mentions, and structured detail past the cut. Both demand front-loading the value prop.
