YouTube and Meta deliver video ads with fundamentally different mental models. YouTube treats video as the destination — viewers chose to be on YouTube, the ad interrupts the chosen content, and the companion banner provides the click-through path. Meta treats video as a mid-feed disruption — viewers are scrolling, the autoplay video competes with the next post for attention, and primary text plus headline carry the persuasion job.

Character economics reflect the difference. YouTube companion banner: 45 visible headline chars + 60 visible description chars = ~105 visible characters total. Meta Reels primary text: 72 visible chars + 10-char headline = ~82 visible characters total. Meta is tighter on the headline, more generous on intro text. YouTube is the inverse.
