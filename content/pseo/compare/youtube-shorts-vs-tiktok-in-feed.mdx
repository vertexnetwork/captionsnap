YouTube Shorts and TikTok In-Feed are the two largest short-form vertical video ad surfaces, and they are visually nearly identical. Both run a full-bleed 9:16 video with a right-rail engagement column, a username block bottom-left, and a sponsored CTA strip across the bottom. The character clamps diverge at exactly the visible caption window.

TikTok In-Feed clips caption at roughly 100 visible characters. YouTube Shorts clips description at roughly 60 visible characters — a 40% tighter window. The difference comes from YouTube's right-rail having six icons (like, dislike, comment, share, remix, subscribe) versus TikTok's five (like, comment, share, bookmark, audio). YouTube also embeds a "Subscribe" pill in the channel block that TikTok's @username row doesn't have.

For cross-platform creative, plan to YouTube's tighter ceiling. A 60-character caption fits both; a 100-character TikTok-optimized caption will get clipped on Shorts.
