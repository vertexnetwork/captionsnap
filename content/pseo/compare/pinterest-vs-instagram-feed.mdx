Pinterest Pin Ads and Instagram Feed Ads are visually similar — both are vertical-leaning image formats inside a feed — but the user mental models could not be more different. Instagram users are in a "what's happening now" mode (scroll, react, move on). Pinterest users are in a "save for later" mode (browse, save, return).

The character economics reflect the difference. Pinterest title clamps at 40 visible chars; description at ~50 visible on mobile. Instagram primary text clamps at 125 visible. Instagram is more generous on body text because the platform expects in-feed reading. Pinterest is tight on title because the title functions more like a search-result H1 than feed copy.

Aspect ratios also diverge. Pinterest favors 2:3 vertical (1000×1500). Instagram Feed favors 1:1 square. Pin Ads cropped from Instagram Feed creatives lose composition; plan separate visual treatments.
