Meta Reels and TikTok In-Feed are the closest direct comparison in the social ad ecosystem — both 9:16 vertical, both fast-scroll, both designed to feel native to short-form video culture. The character math diverges in one important way: Meta clamps Reels primary text at 72 visible characters, while TikTok shows ~100. That 28-character gap is the difference between "tight punchy hook" and "tight punchy hook plus payoff."

Headline behavior also diverges. Meta Reels has a 10-character visible headline window — yes, ten — designed for ultra-short labels. TikTok's 40-character display name doesn't visually truncate but does affect caption block compression past 30 characters.

Safe zones are similar but not identical. Both have right-rail engagement columns. Meta Reels' column extends 30%–80% Y (50% tall). TikTok's extends 28%–82% Y (54% tall). Both have bottom username/caption blocks; TikTok's is taller. Both have a sponsored CTA strip at the bottom.

## Cross-platform safe budget

If you're writing one piece of copy for both surfaces, your safe budget is 60 visible characters and 10 characters for headline (Meta Reels' tightest constraint). Anything past those numbers won't survive both surfaces.
