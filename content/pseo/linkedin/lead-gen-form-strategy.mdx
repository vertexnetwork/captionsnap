LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms attach to any Sponsored Content format — single image, video, carousel, or document — and pre-fill from the user's LinkedIn profile when they click the CTA. Because every field is auto-filled, completion rates are an order of magnitude higher than custom landing-page forms (typical 12-20% vs 2-5%), but only if the form stays short. LinkedIn caps the form at 12 fields total. The platform's own data shows a steep completion-rate cliff past field 5: a 5-field form typically closes at 15-20%, a 9-field form drops to 6-8%.

Character limits on the form itself: form name 256 chars (admin-only), offer headline 60 chars (visible), offer detail 160 chars, privacy policy URL plus 2,000 chars of optional custom terms. The body of the ad inherits its parent format's limits — single image, video, carousel, or document.

## Field-count rule of thumb

Every field past 5 cuts completion rate by roughly 1.5 percentage points. If you're collecting more than 5 fields, you should be running a custom landing page instead — the qualification gain isn't worth the funnel collapse.
