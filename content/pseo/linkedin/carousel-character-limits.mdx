LinkedIn Carousel Ads use a tighter intro-text limit than the rest of Sponsored Content: 255 characters total instead of 600, with the same ~150 visible "see more" clamp. The reasoning from LinkedIn is that carousels carry their own narrative inside the cards, so the intro text only needs to set up the swipe — but in practice the 255-character cap punishes media buyers who try to ship single-image and carousel variations from the same copy doc. Each card has its own headline (45 characters max) and the carousel itself supports between 2 and 10 cards. There is no description field on cards.

The interesting structural difference vs Meta carousel: LinkedIn renders the carousel arrow on the right edge at roughly 88% X for the first 30% of the card height, which means a per-card hero image with a right-aligned product needs to clear that arrow zone. Plan creative with the central 70% of the card width as the safe canvas.

## Card-by-card strategy

Each card headline is its own 45-character commitment. Treat the carousel as a 5-frame storyboard: card 1 = hook, cards 2–4 = proof points or product variants, card 5 = CTA. Avoid generic numbering ("1. Why we're better") — the headline space is too tight to waste.
