LinkedIn Video Ads inherit the Sponsored Content shell but trade the static creative for autoplay video. The platform allows 700 characters of introductory text — 100 more than the single-image allowance — under the assumption that video viewers will linger longer in the post region after the auto-play hooks them. In practice, the visible "see more" clamp does not change: roughly 150 characters on mobile, 210 on desktop. The extra 100 characters of intro headroom only matters for the audience that taps "see more" after watching the video.

Headlines accept up to 70 characters with about 50 visible on mobile (a slightly more generous window than single-image because the video occupies more vertical real estate, leaving less room for the bottom CTA strip to truncate). The most expensive mistake on LinkedIn video is letting the video do all the work: viewers with autoplay-with-sound disabled will only read the intro text, and 80% of LinkedIn impressions happen with sound off.

## What changed in 2026

LinkedIn now penalizes video ads with intro text shorter than 80 characters in the relevance score — short intros are treated as low-effort and demoted in delivery. The 700-character ceiling has not changed since 2024.
