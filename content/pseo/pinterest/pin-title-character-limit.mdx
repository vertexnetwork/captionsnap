The Pinterest pin title field accepts **100 characters**, but only the **first ~40 render in the mobile feed** before the bottom CTA strip clips the rest. This is true for both organic Pins and Promoted (ad) Pins — the title field and its truncation point are identical; promotion does not extend it. If your keyword phrase or hook sits past character 40, feed users never see it; they only see it on the Pin detail (close-up) view after a tap.

This is the single most misunderstood Pinterest spec, because the 100-character ceiling is what the upload form and most "best practices" posts quote — while the number that actually governs whether your title works is the ~40-character *visible* window.

## Pin title best practices that follow from the cutoff

- **Put the search keyword in the first ~40 characters.** Pinterest is a visual search engine; the title is a ranking and matching signal. A title whose keyword lands at character 60 is both invisible in-feed *and* weaker for Pinterest search.
- **Write a complete thought by character 40.** "Cream merino crew sweater under $80" (35 chars) reads fully in-feed. "The coziest, softest, most beautiful cream merino crew neck sweater you'll want for fall" clips to "The coziest, softest, most beautiful…" — pure waste.
- **Use the remaining 60 characters for long-tail SEO,** not persuasion. Those characters help Pinterest and Google image search match the Pin even though feed users won't read them.

## Does the limit change in 2026?

No. The 100-character title maximum and ~40-character feed truncation are unchanged for 2026 across Standard, Video, and Carousel Pin formats. Idea Ad page-overlay text follows a different rule — see the related pages below.
