Pinterest Idea Ads (formerly Story Pins) are multi-page vertical formats — between 3 and 20 pages per Idea — designed to mirror the Stories pattern from Instagram, but adapted for Pinterest's save-and-discover behavior. The title field caps at 100 chars (~40 visible on mobile feed). Per-page text overlays sit on top of the creative and clip at roughly 100 visible characters before truncation, matching Pinterest's broader description-line clamp.

Idea Ads carry a multi-page progress bar across the top of the screen that covers the top 6% — tighter than Stories' 14% because Pinterest does not surface a profile chip in the same place. The actor row (pinner name + Promoted label) sits in the next 8% below the progress bar. Plan creative with the central 78% of the canvas as the primary safe zone for product placement.

## Page-by-page narrative

Idea Ads work as a 5-7 page narrative storyboard. Page 1 = hook. Pages 2-5 = step-by-step demonstration. Final page = CTA + product link. Skipping the storyboard structure and dropping repeated product photos performs poorly.
