Meta Dynamic Creative auto-mixes your creative inputs into multiple ad variations. You can supply up to 5 primary texts, 5 headlines, and 5 descriptions per ad, and Meta combines them in real time based on user data. The character limits per input are identical to Feed — 500-character primary, 255-character headline, 200-character description — with the same 125 / 40 / 30 visible windows.

The trap with Dynamic Creative is forgetting that every variation has to hit the per-field limit independently. If you supply five primary texts and one of them is 132 visible characters, that variation will clamp on every impression. Half your tested variants will look broken. The platform doesn't warn you. It just runs them.

The smart pattern is to write all variations to the worst-case character budget. Write every primary text to 110 visible characters or less. Write every headline to 27 characters or less. Write every description to 27 characters or less. The discipline costs you a little creative ceiling but it ensures none of your variations clamp.

## Per-variant discipline

Treat the 5×5×5 grid as 125 unique combinations. Each combination has to look clean. The math says any one variation that clamps weakens 25 of the 125 combinations. That's a 20% degradation in visual quality from a single oversize input.
