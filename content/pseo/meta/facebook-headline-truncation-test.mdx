This page is a live test, not a spec. Paste your real ad headline into the simulator below, and watch in real time where it cuts off across Facebook Feed, Marketplace, and Carousel placements. The visible-character window changes per placement, and the cutoff is enforced visually by the platform — not just numerically — so a 39-character headline that "fits" on Feed may still clip on Marketplace.

The most common surprise is that Carousel ads use the same 40-character visible window as Feed for the headline, but the description field's window collapses from Feed's 30 down to Carousel's 20. So a card-level headline that survives gets its description neatly cropped. Marketplace introduces its own variance — same 40-character window for headline, but the surrounding price tag shrinks the perceived width.

## Why this preview matters

Most ad managers preview your ad in a simulated desktop window where the headline rarely clips. That preview lies. The real-world clip happens on phones with narrower windows. The simulator above defaults to a 390 logical-pixel iPhone-class device, which is the worst case for most cohorts.
