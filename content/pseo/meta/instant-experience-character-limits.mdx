Meta Instant Experience (formerly Canvas) is a full-screen mobile landing page that opens when the user taps a Feed, Reels, or Stories ad. It is composed of blocks — header, photos, videos, carousels, buttons, product tiles, text — and each block has its own copy budget.

Header blocks support 30 characters of title. Text blocks support up to 500 characters with a recommended 150 for readability. Button blocks support 30 characters of label. Product blocks inherit the Collection Ads tile budget (30 characters). The total Instant Experience can have up to 20 blocks.

The format is powerful but error-prone because every block has different limits. The fix is to plan the entire experience as a sequence of micro-pages, each with the per-block budget at the top of the spec. Don't write Instant Experience copy as flowing prose — write it as discrete blocks.

## Block-level discipline

Every block is a mini-design problem. Plan each block's copy before laying out the experience. Don't try to retrofit a long article into Instant Experience — it will fragment in ways that hurt comprehension.
