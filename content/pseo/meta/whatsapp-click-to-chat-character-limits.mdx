Click-to-WhatsApp ads route a user from a Meta placement directly into a WhatsApp conversation. The ad creative inherits Feed limits (125 visible primary text, 40 visible headline). The differentiator is the pre-filled message — the text that opens automatically in WhatsApp when the user taps the CTA. That pre-filled message caps at 256 characters.

Pre-filled messages are easy to underestimate. They look like a small detail, but they are the first message in the conversation. They set the tone, frame what the user is asking about, and determine how the receiving sales agent responds. A 256-character pre-fill that's unclear gets routed to the wrong queue. A clear, specific pre-fill gets a fast, relevant reply.

The recommended pattern is "[product name] inquiry" plus a single specific question. Example: "I'm interested in the Studio Bag. Is it available in black?" That's 60 characters and routes cleanly. Avoid generic openers — they're indistinguishable from spam in the receiving queue.

## The pre-fill discipline

256 characters is plenty for a useful pre-fill. Most successful campaigns use 60–120 characters and include the specific product or query. Don't waste the pre-fill on greetings.
