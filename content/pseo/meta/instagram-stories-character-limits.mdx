Instagram Stories ads have the tightest character limits in Meta's catalog. Primary text caps at 125 characters total — not 125 visible, 125 hard. Headline caps at 40 characters total. Descriptions are not used on Stories.

Why so tight? Stories are a sealed-slot format. The user has 5 to 15 seconds before the next Story rolls. There is no scroll-back, no See more affordance, no ability to expand the text. The platform built its character clamps around the actual time a viewer can spend reading, and the answer is "very little."

This means Stories ads must be written in a fundamentally different style than Feed ads. There is no warm-up, no proof, no closing argument. The 125-character primary text is the entire ad message. Most successful Stories ad copy fits in 70–90 characters and uses the rest as breathing room.

## Sealed-slot discipline

Treat Stories like a 6-second commercial. Hook, payoff, CTA — that's the entire ad. Anything you write past 90 characters is a luxury most viewers won't read.
