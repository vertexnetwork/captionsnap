Facebook Marketplace ads sit inside the Marketplace tab, blending paid placements with organic listings. The character limits mirror Feed almost exactly — 500-character primary text, 255-character headline, 200-character description, with 125/40/30 visible windows respectively. The contextual difference is what matters: your headline is competing with price tags from real listings rather than other ads.

This changes the writing job. On Feed, your headline competes with other professional ad headlines for attention. On Marketplace, your headline competes with "$45 — Used IKEA Bookshelf — Brooklyn" — short, specific, transactional. A 40-character branded headline that performs well on Feed often underperforms next to organic Marketplace copy because it doesn't speak the surface's native language.

The most effective Marketplace headlines lead with a price or a quantity. "From $9 — fits in a backpack" feels native. "Discover the next generation of fitness" feels like an ad. Both fit in 40 characters; only one earns the tap.

## Native-mode copy
