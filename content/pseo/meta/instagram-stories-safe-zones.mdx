Instagram Stories ads run inside a sealed 9:16 slot. Unlike Feed, where the UI sits above and below your creative, Stories overlays UI directly on top of your visual. The progress bar and profile chip cover the top 14% of the canvas, and the swipe-up sticker, CTA button, and reply field eat the bottom 14%. That leaves a usable design region of roughly 72% of the vertical canvas.

Designers who build Stories the same way they build Feed inevitably lose product-shot real estate. A logo placed in the top-right "for branding" gets covered by the progress dots. A CTA button overlaid in the bottom third gets covered by the actual sticker CTA. The platform makes no effort to compose around your creative — it composes on top of it.

Worse, the sealed-slot nature of Stories means there is no scroll. The viewer has 5 to 15 seconds to absorb the message before the next ad rolls in. Type-heavy designs underperform on Stories because the viewer doesn't have time to read in detail.

## The 14/72/14 rule

Build every Stories creative against the 14/72/14 vertical split: 14% sacrificial top, 72% usable middle, 14% sacrificial bottom. Anything you place in the sacrificial bands must survive being half-covered. Anything in the middle 72% is yours.
